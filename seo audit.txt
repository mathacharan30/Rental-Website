  Critical Issues

  1. JavaScript SPA — Content Not Server-Side Rendered

  Impact: Blocks full indexation of all pages

  The site is a client-side JavaScript application (likely React). When pages are fetched statically, only the <title> tag is visible — all H1/H2
  headings, body content, product details, NAP data, images, and internal links are rendered by JavaScript in the browser.

  Why this matters: Google uses a two-pass crawling system. It indexes the raw HTML first, then queues pages for JavaScript rendering — which can
  take days to weeks. Any content only in the JS bundle may not be indexed promptly or reliably. Competitors with server-rendered HTML have a
  significant advantage.

  Recommended fix: Migrate to Next.js with SSR or SSG (Server-Side Rendering or Static Site Generation). Since most content on service pages is
  static, SSG is ideal and free on Vercel. Product pages with dynamic inventory should use ISR (Incremental Static Regeneration).

  ---
  2. Four Pages Returning 404 — Listed in Sitemap

  Impact: Crawl budget waste + index errors in Google Search Console

  The following URLs are in sitemap.xml but return HTTP 404:

  - /photography-services-mysuru
  - /photography-services-bangalore
  - /bridal-package-mysuru
  - /bridal-package-bangalore

  These are important high-intent pages (photography services and bridal packages are core offerings). Google is wasting crawl budget hitting these,
  and they will show as errors in Search Console.

  Recommended fix:
  - Option A (Better): Create these pages with proper content. Photography services and bridal packages are high-value keywords.
  - Option B (Interim): Remove these URLs from sitemap.xml immediately. If they will be created soon, add them back once live.
  - Never leave 404 pages in a sitemap.

  improving click-through rates.

  Recommended meta descriptions (write one per page, 150–160 characters):

  High Priority Issues

  4. No Schema Markup on Any Page

  Impact: No rich results, no local pack eligibility signals, no AI citation signals

  Not a single page has JSON-LD structured data. For a local bridal studio, this is a large missed opportunity.

  Required schema by page type:

  Homepage + Contact page — LocalBusiness schema:
  {
    "@context": "https://schema.org",
    "@type": "LocalBusiness",
    "name": "People & Style",
    "description": "Bridal rental studio offering clothing, jewellery, makeup and photography in Mysuru and Bangalore",
    "url": "https://www.peopleandstyle.in",
    "telephone": "+91-XXXXXXXXXX",
    "address": [
      {
        "@type": "PostalAddress",
        "addressLocality": "Mysuru",
        "addressRegion": "Karnataka",
        "addressCountry": "IN"
      }
    ],
    "areaServed": ["Mysuru", "Bangalore"],
    "sameAs": [
      "https://www.instagram.com/peopleandstyle",
      "https://www.facebook.com/peopleandstyle"
    ]
  }

  Service pages — Service schema: Each service page (rental-clothing, rental-jewellery, makeup-services, photography-services) should have a Service
  schema with areaServed, provider, and serviceType.

  Product pages — Product schema: 60 product pages need Product schema with name, description, image, offers (with price, priceCurrency,
  availability), and brand.

  FAQ page — FAQPage schema: Valuable for AI/LLM citations even though Google no longer shows FAQ rich results for commercial sites.

  5. Photography Services Pages — Core Offering Completely Missing

  Impact: Zero organic traffic for photography-related searches

  Both /photography-services-mysuru and /photography-services-bangalore return 404. Photography is a key service offering (listed in the FAQ page
  title). There is zero SEO presence for search terms like:
  - "bridal photographer Mysuru"
  - "wedding photography Mysuru"
  - "bridal photography Bangalore"

  These are high-intent, commercial searches. Without these pages, all that traffic goes to competitors.

  ---
  6. Bridal Package Pages Missing

  Impact: Missing high-conversion bundle pages

  /bridal-package-mysuru and /bridal-package-bangalore return 404. "Bridal package" searches are high-intent transactional queries from users ready
  to book. A bundle page combining clothing + jewellery + makeup + photography is a strong conversion page.

  ---
  7. FAQ Page Missing in Both Locations

  Impact: Weaker FAQ page with incomplete geographic coverage

  The FAQ page title reads: FAQ | People & Style — Bridal Rental, Makeup & Photography Mysuru — Bangalore is missing from the title. Since the
  business serves both cities, the FAQ title should reflect both.

  Suggested: FAQ | People & Style — Bridal Rental FAQs for Mysuru & Bangalore

  ---
  Medium Priority Issues

  8. Title Tag Inconsistency — "Bridal" vs "Wedding" Keyword Gap

  The site exclusively uses "bridal" in titles. Many users search "wedding" instead:
  - "wedding dress on rent Mysuru" — not targeted
  - "wedding jewellery rental Bangalore" — not targeted

  Recommended fix: Add "wedding" variants in H1/H2 headings and body copy on service pages (not duplicate title tags — use the headings and body text
   for variants).

  ---
  9. Products Page Missing City Segmentation

  /products serves all products together for both cities. Users from Mysuru want to see Mysuru stock, not Bangalore. Consider:
  - /products?city=mysuru with canonical handling
  - Or separate /products-mysuru and /products-bangalore pages with location-specific title tags and filtered inventory

  ---
  10. Product Pages — Title Pattern Needs Location Specificity

  Current product page title: "Rani Pink Bridal Lehenga — Rent in Mysuru & Bangalore | People & Style"

  If a product is available in only one city, the title should reflect that. If available in both, this is fine. But if 60 products have the same
  "Mysuru & Bangalore" suffix, it dilutes location relevance signals.

  ---
  11. Sitemap Contains No Image Sitemaps

  With 60+ product pages featuring images of outfits, an image sitemap or image tags within the existing sitemap would help Google index product
  images for Google Images search — a potentially significant traffic source for visual bridal content.

  ---
  12. No llms.txt File

  Impact: AI search tools (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini) less likely to reference the site

  There is no /llms.txt file. With AI search assistants increasingly answering "bridal rental in Mysuru" queries, a llms.txt helps AI crawlers
  understand the site's services, locations, and expertise.

  Suggested /llms.txt:
  # People & Style — Bridal Rental Studio
  > Bridal clothing, jewellery, makeup and photography rental services in Mysuru and Bangalore, Karnataka, India.

  ## Services
  - Bridal clothing rental (lehengas, gowns, sherwanis)
  - Bridal jewellery rental (temple, kundan, gold sets)
  - Bridal makeup (HD, airbrush, traditional)
  - Bridal photography

  ## Locations
  - Mysuru, Karnataka
  - Bangalore, Karnataka

  ## Website
  - https://www.peopleandstyle.in

  ---
  13. No Canonical Tags Confirmed

  Because the site is JS-rendered, canonical tag presence cannot be verified. If /products?city=mysuru and /products serve similar content without
  canonicalization, duplicate content issues can arise.

  ---
  Low Priority Issues

  14. Robots.txt — Minor Improvements

  Current robots.txt is well-structured. One addition to consider: explicitly allow the /product/ path to ensure all 60 product pages are crawlable.

  15. Hreflang Not Present

  The site serves Indian users in Karnataka but uses English. No hreflang needed at this stage. If Kannada-language pages are created in the future,
  hreflang tags will be required.

  16. No Blog / Content Hub

  There is no content hub targeting informational queries like:
  - "how to choose bridal lehenga Mysuru"
  - "bridal jewellery rental tips"
  - "wedding photography checklist Karnataka"

  These queries are top-of-funnel and build organic authority. A small blog (5–10 articles) can drive significant traffic from brides in early
  planning stages.
